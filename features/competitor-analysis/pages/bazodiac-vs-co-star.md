## Detailed Comparison

### Astrological Systems

**Co-Star** uses Western tropical astrology exclusively. It shows your Sun, Moon, and Rising signs, some house placements, and generates daily readings based on transits. This is the same system used by most Western astrology apps.

**Bazodiac** layers three independent systems:
- **Western**: Sun, Moon, Ascendant, all 12 houses with sign placements and keywords
- **BaZi (Chinese Four Pillars)**: Year, Month, Day, and Hour pillars — each containing a heavenly stem, earthly branch, animal, and element
- **WuXing (Five Elements)**: Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water distribution from your BaZi chart

Then, Bazodiac's AI **cross-references all three systems** in a single interpretation. This fusion reveals connections invisible to any single system — like how your Western Moon sign's emotional energy maps to your BaZi Day Master's elemental nature.

**Bottom line**: Co-Star gives you one perspective on your birth chart. Bazodiac gives you three and synthesises them.

### Daily Use vs. Birth Chart Focus

**Co-Star** is designed for daily engagement. Push notifications arrive each morning with your horoscope. You check it, compare with friends, share a screenshot. It's a daily ritual.

**Bazodiac** is designed for depth exploration. Your birth chart (across all three systems) is your permanent cosmic portrait. The 15+ personality quizzes feed into a "Fusion Ring" that evolves over time. There are no daily horoscopes — your chart doesn't change, so Bazodiac focuses on helping you understand it fully.

**Bottom line**: If you want a daily astrology habit, Co-Star. If you want a deep dive you return to when you're seeking understanding, Bazodiac.
